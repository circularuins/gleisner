# Gleisner — Concept

## Peek into an artist's mind. Explore. Support.

Gleisner is a lifelog platform where fans can peek into the overflowing creativity of an artist's mind, explore their creative journey, and support them along the way.

While existing social networks serve as stages to "showcase finished work," Gleisner is **a space to record and share the creative thought process itself**.

## Differentiation as a Lifelog

### Gleisner is NOT a media hosting service

High-quality, unlimited media hosting is a domain already dominated by YouTube and Instagram. Gleisner does not compete there.

Gleisner's core is **lifelog** — structuring an artist's activities chronologically and visualizing them as constellations in virtual space. No existing service has established this niche.

### Ideal Use Cases

1. **Post everyday ideas casually** — Record thoughts freely without worrying about follower reactions
2. **Constellations form naturally** — As posts accumulate, the artist's activity becomes visible as constellations in virtual space
3. **Connect with external services** — Post polished videos to YouTube, tracks to SoundCloud, then link them to Gleisner's lifelog via URL-type posts
4. **Fans travel through constellations** — Fans trace the flow of an artist's thoughts, creative process, and daily fragments, immersing themselves in the artist's world

### Media as Equal Citizens

Video, audio, and images are simply **one of several media types**. A text thought, a shared link, a photo snap, a short performance clip, a voice memo — all are treated equally as "fragments of a lifelog."

| Media Type | Role in Gleisner | Limit |
|-----------|-----------------|-------|
| Text | Daily thoughts, idea memos | None |
| Image | Snaps, sketches, sheet music | None |
| Video | Short performance clips, studio footage | 1 minute max (short video) |
| Audio | Voice memos, demo recordings | 5 minutes max |
| URL | Interface to external content | None |

### The Strategic Importance of URL Type

URL type is not mere link sharing. It serves as **an interface to externally hosted media data**.

> "For videos over 1 minute, post them on YouTube. For audio over 5 minutes, post them on SoundCloud. Then share the link on Gleisner as a URL-type post."

This allows users to build a complete lifelog without consuming Gleisner's free storage quota. Since content resides on external services, it falls outside Gleisner's "protection from unjust deletion" guarantee — but it functions as a practical approach to using Gleisner for free.

For users who want data permanence, they can pay for Gleisner's premium plan or set up their own decentralized storage in the future — the user decides their own strategy.

## Greg Egan's "Diaspora" Philosophy

Gleisner's design philosophy is rooted in Greg Egan's science fiction novel "Diaspora."

| Principle | Egan's World | Application in Gleisner |
|-----------|-------------|----------------------|
| **Self-determination** | Polis citizens freely choose their own consciousness structure | Users decide their own data, storage, and privacy settings |
| **Diversity of existence** | Polis citizens, Gleisners, and Fleshers coexist | Text, images, video, audio, and URLs coexist as equals |
| **Resistance to annihilation** | Digital citizens are never "deleted" | Lifelogs persist. Freedom from unjust bans |
| **Exploration** | Pure curiosity drives journeys to the edge of the universe | Fans travel through artist constellations, exploring the source of creation |
| **Structural beauty** | Mathematically elegant laws of the universe | DAW-style multitrack timeline × constellation visualization |

## Evolution by Phase

Media limitations will be relaxed as the service matures:

- **Phase 0 (Family)**: No media limits (test operation)
- **Phase 1 (Invite-only)**: Video 1min, audio 5min limits; per-user quota introduced
- **Phase 2 (Public)**: Paid plans to relax limits; server-side video compression
- **Future**: Native apps may relax time limits; decentralized storage support (users choose their own storage provider)
