# ADR 002: Naming — "Gleisner"

## Status

Accepted

## Context

The project needs a name that reflects its core purpose and aligns with the yatima umbrella's naming convention: drawing inspiration from Greg Egan's science fiction, particularly *Diaspora*.

## Decision

The project is named **Gleisner**, after the **Gleisner robots** in Greg Egan's *Diaspora*.

### Why Gleisner?

In Egan's universe, Gleisner robots are physical-world embodiments of digital citizens. They are the bridge between the computational world of the polises and the tangible, physical reality. Gleisner robots allow digital beings to interact with, explore, and affect the physical world.

This maps directly to the project's purpose:

| Egan's Gleisner robots | This project |
|------------------------|--------------|
| Bridge digital citizens to physical reality | Bridge an artist's physical-world creative activities to their digital presence |
| Autonomous bodies controlled by their owner | A platform controlled by the artist, not a corporation |
| Enable exploration of the physical world | Enable audiences to explore the full breadth of an artist's work |
| Exist independently of any single polis | Exist independently of any single platform |

The name also fits within the yatima umbrella (named after the novel's protagonist), establishing a coherent thematic lineage.

## Consequences

- The name carries deep thematic meaning that can guide design decisions (physical-digital bridging, autonomy, independence).
- Contributors familiar with Egan's work will immediately grasp the project's philosophical underpinnings.
- The name is distinctive and unlikely to conflict with existing projects.
